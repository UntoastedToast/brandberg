Brandberg

The Brandberg massif (often simply Brandberg; khoekhoegowab Dâures, otjiherero Omukuruvaro) is a mountain massif in Damaraland in Namibia. It is located in the Erongo region in the west of the country, about 90 km from the Atlantic Ocean, is 2500 m high on average and towers over the surrounding land by almost 2000 m. Its and at the same time Namibia's highest mountain is the Königstein with a height of 2573 m. The Zisab peak (2228 m) on the eastern edge of the Brandberg massif also has an exposed position. The entire elevation is oval and occupies an area of 420 km².

Overview

Located between 21° and 21°15′ south latitude and 14°25′ and 14°40′ east longitude, Brandberg is 80-90 km from Cape Cross and the Atlantic coast. Covering an area of about 420 km² (750 km² by other accounts), it forms a slightly oval massif 26 × 21 km in extent and 23 km in mean diameter, towering above its surroundings by an average of nearly 2,000 m, and which is the most prominent terrain elevation in the central Namib. The massif is still visible from a distance of more than 100 km on a clear day. The nearest town is the former mining settlement of Uis, which lies about 30 km from the Brandberg.

Name

Early mentions of the Brandberg and its inhabitants date back to the 19th century, for example by Charles John Andersson and Georg Gürich. The report of a "Captain Messum" quoted by Charles John Andersson is probably one of the first mentions of today's Brandberg at all. According to Captain Messum, the mountain, which the locals called "Dourissa", was named "Messum Mountain" or "Mount Messum" by its discoverer. The mountain - with an estimated elevation of 3200 feet above sea level - is also marked on a map published in 1856. Although the name did not catch on, Capitain Messum's name lives on in the designation for the "Messum Crater" located 45 km to the southwest.

On a map of German Southwest Africa in Langhans' colonial atlas published in 1894, the Brandberg massif appears with three different names: "Brandberg", "Daureb" and "Omukuruwaro".

The official name Brandberg, still used today, comes from the glowing color in which the mountain appears when the sun shines on it from the west. The Khoisan (Khoekhoegowab) speaking Damara, whose settlement area has always included the Brandberg, call it Daureb, Daunas or Daures, which translates as "burnt mountain", "because there the land is so bare and without grass or tree, as if it had been burnt (daú)". The Bantu (Otjiherero) speaking Herero, who invaded Damaraland from the middle of the 18th century, coming from the Kaokoveld, refer to the Brandberg as Omukuruwaro (Mountain of the Gods) or The Land/Area of the Ancients/Ancestors.

In more recent literature, it is referred to as Dâures Mountain.
